Tire and Rim Association (TRA) Classifications

TRA Codes are standard international codes defining OTR tyres. The prefix letter indicates vehicle category (E = Earthmover/Hauler, L = Loader/Dozer, G = Grader, C = Compactor). The trailing number specifies tread depth ratios relative to a standard casing:

TRA Code Tread Type Tread Depth Ratio Typical Application
E2 Traction Radial Regular (100%) Dump trucks, scrapers on soft dirt terrains.
E3 Rock Radial Regular (100%) General haul roads, open-cut mining hauler.
E4 Rock Deep 150% Deep Tread Heavy haulage standard. Denser protection under loads.
G2 Traction Regular (100%) Directional traction grading operations.
L3 Rock Loader Regular (100%) Balanced loading and carry speeds. Cut resistant.
L4 Rock Deep Loader 150% Deep Tread Loader dozer rock loading in abrasive pit yards.
L5 Rock Ultra Deep 250% Deep Tread Severe, jagged rock yards. Maximum cut defense.
L5S Smooth Ultra Deep 250% Smooth block Underground mining LHD. Zero grooves to prevent shard entry.

Technical OTR Glossary

TKPH (Ton-Kilometer-Per-Hour)

Thermal safety index carrying-capacity rating representing internal heat limits. Operating a haul truck above tyre's TKPH will result in rubber casing delamination and blowout.

Work Cycle Factor (WCF)

Loader/Dozer thermal index representing speed-load stress during loading or load-and-carry operations.

Star Rating (★)

Standard index rating representing radial steel casing strength. Star ratings (★, ★★, ★★★) correspond to traditional Bias ply equivalence indices.

Bias Ply Casing

Traditional multi-ply cross diagonal cords construction. Extremely stiff sidewalls ideal for side sway loader cycles, but generates high heat at speed.

Dual Axle Tyre Mixing Regulations

Placing tyres with mismatched diameters or ratings on the same dual assembly axle is dangerous. The larger tyre will carry the majority of the weight load, causing rapid heat buildup and premature casing blowouts.

Axle Placement Standards:
  • ▪ Diameter Match: Never exceed 1% total diameter difference between dual-mounted tyres.
  • ▪ Tread Pattern Match: Always dual-mount identical TRA codes on the same axle (e.g. do not mix E3 and E4).
  • ▪ Casing Type Match: Radial and Bias tyres must **never** be mixed on the same axle.

Tyre Construction: Radial vs Bias

The mechanical carcass construction dictates OTR performance bounds:

Radial steel plies

Casing steel cables run at 90-degrees relative to rotation. Offers low rolling resistance, flexible sidewall tread contact, low thermal heat buildup at high speeds, and optimal B2B fuel savings.

Bias diagonal plies

Cross diagonal nylon layers. Provides rigid sidewalls to resist lateral forces and side-sway load stresses during digging cycles. Highly abrasive cut defense but suffers rapid heat vulcanization.
